# Scheme Sahayak — Problem Statement & Approach

**Idea2Impact Online Hackathon 2026 · Theme 1: Sustainability & Social Impact**
Domains: Social Welfare · Financial Inclusion · Public Services · Rural Development

## 1. The Problem

The Government of India operates hundreds of central welfare schemes with lakhs of crores in allocated benefits — income support for farmers (PM-KISAN), ₹5-lakh health cover (Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY), pensions for widows, the elderly and persons with disabilities (NSAP), scholarships, maternity benefits, and micro-credit for street vendors and artisans.

Yet a large share of eligible citizens never claim these benefits. The reasons are consistent across studies of welfare uptake:

**Awareness gap.** A daily-wage worker in a village has no reliable way to learn that a scheme exists for her, let alone that she qualifies. Information lives on dozens of separate English-first portals.

**Language and literacy barrier.** Scheme documentation is written in bureaucratic English. Over 88% of Indians do not speak English; guidance in Hindi, Telugu and other languages is fragmented or absent.

**Complexity of eligibility.** Rules cross-cut age, income, gender, occupation, category, residence and life events. A widowed woman farmer aged 45 with a BPL card may qualify for six different schemes — but no single source tells her that.

**No guided next step.** Even citizens who discover a scheme don't know which documents to prepare or where to apply, so they abandon the process or pay middlemen.

The cost is not abstract: unclaimed pensions, uninsured hospitalisations that push families into debt, and credit-starved micro-entrepreneurs — precisely the people these schemes were designed for.

## 2. Who It Affects

Rural and low-income households, women (widow pensions, Ujjwala, maternity benefits are chronically under-claimed), senior citizens, persons with disabilities, street vendors, artisans, and first-generation students. In short: the citizens with the least ability to navigate bureaucratic complexity are asked to navigate the most of it.

## 3. Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

The government's own myscheme.gov.in is a genuine step forward but remains a search-and-browse portal: the citizen must still read long eligibility pages, mostly in English, and self-assess. Private "sarkari yojana" websites are ad-farms with outdated, unverified information. Neither offers personalised, conversational, multilingual guidance — and a plain LLM chatbot is dangerous here, because hallucinated eligibility or benefit amounts cause real harm to vulnerable people.

## 4. Our Approach: Rules Decide, AI Explains

Scheme Sahayak splits the problem the way it should be split:

**Eligibility is a compliance decision → deterministic rules engine.** We curated 21 major central schemes into a structured dataset (eligibility criteria, benefits, documents, official links). A tested matching engine evaluates a citizen's 11-field profile against every scheme and produces explainable, reproducible results with per-criterion reasons. No hallucination is possible in the decision path. Targeted schemes rank above universal ones via a specificity score.

**Explanation and guidance is a language problem → Gemini.** For the matched schemes, Gemini 2.5 Flash (server-side) generates a personal summary, per-scheme "why you qualify", 3–4 concrete application steps, and document tips — in the citizen's chosen language (English, Hindi, Telugu), grounded strictly in the engine's output via schema-constrained JSON prompting. A follow-up chat answers practical questions ("Where do I get an income certificate?") grounded only in the citizen's matched schemes, with safe redirection to myscheme.gov.in and helplines when out of scope.

**Trust by design.** Nothing is stored — the profile lives only in the request. Every card links to the official government portal, and the UI explicitly labels what was decided by rules versus generated by AI. If the AI layer fails, verified matches still render.

## 5. Impact

A one-minute, no-login check in the citizen's own language converts "I've vaguely heard of some scheme" into "I qualify for these five schemes; here is my document list and my first step." The same architecture scales horizontally — add state schemes to the dataset, add languages by changing one prompt line, and deploy through CSC operators, ASHA workers and NGO field staff who already mediate scheme access for millions.

## 6. Deliverables

Deployed web app (Vercel) · public GitHub repository with tested code · demo video. Stack: React + Vite, Vercel serverless functions, Gemini 2.5 Flash, custom eligibility engine with 9 unit tests.
